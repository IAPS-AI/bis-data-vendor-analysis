## About WireScreen

### The Organization

[WireScreen](https://wirescreen.ai/) is a New York-based intelligence platform specializing in Chinese corporate data, founded in 2019 as a division of The Wire Digital Inc. Unlike the other vendors in BIS's supply chain intelligence portfolio:

- **Primary focus**: China-specific corporate intelligence
- **Core mission**: Mapping Chinese business networks, ownership structures, and government/military affiliations
- **Method**: Open-source intelligence (OSINT) aggregation from 50+ primary sources including corporate records, government documents, financial filings, and commercial transactions
- **Output**: Searchable database of 14+ million Chinese organizations and 25+ million executives/stakeholders

### The Founders

**David Barboza (Co-founder & CEO)** is a Pulitzer Prize-winning journalist who spent 12 years as The New York Times' Shanghai bureau chief (2004-2016). His investigative work exposed:

> "Billions in secret wealth owned by relatives of [China's] prime minister"

This reporting earned him:
- 2013 Pulitzer Prize for International Reporting
- George Polk Award for Foreign Reporting
- Two Gerald Loeb Awards for Business Reporting

**Lynn Zhang (Co-founder & COO)** is a former private equity financier specializing in Chinese corporate data and due diligence.

The company's technical leadership includes **Leonid Taycher** (Senior Advisor), an MIT PhD and former senior engineer at Google, Twitter, and Kensho (the AI company acquired by S&P Global).

### Funding & Backing

WireScreen has raised approximately **$18 million** in Series A funding, valuing the company at roughly $70 million. Key investors include:

## Why BIS Needs WireScreen: The China Intelligence Gap

### The Problem: Opacity of Chinese Corporate Structures

China's corporate registry system presents unique challenges for export control enforcement:

> "Risk in China hides behind shell companies, renamed entities, nominee directors, and obscured ownership."

Per WireScreen's analysis:
- Chinese entities frequently restructure to obscure military/state connections
- Beneficial ownership chains extend through multiple layers of subsidiaries and joint ventures
- Historical ownership relationships persist across restructurings

### The BIS 50% Affiliates Rule

In late 2025, BIS significantly expanded the effective reach of export controls through the **50% Affiliates Rule**, which subjects any entity majority-owned by an Entity List company to the same export restrictions.

WireScreen's October 2025 analysis found this rule expansion affects **20,000+ Chinese entities** beyond the ~1,300 directly on the Entity List—a **fifteenfold increase** in scope.

Key WireScreen capabilities for this rule:

| Capability | BIS Application |
|------------|-----------------|
| **Identify Affiliates** | Detect direct and indirect ownership crossing 50% threshold |
| **Audit Ownership History** | Track when entities crossed critical thresholds |
| **Automate Risk Flags** | Surface connections to sanctioned/restricted owners |
| **Visualize Networks** | Map relationships between listed entities and subsidiaries |
| **Curated Collections** | Pre-built datasets aligned with Entity List, MEU, SDN 744.8 |

### Complementary to Sayari

While Sayari provides global corporate governance data, WireScreen offers **China-specific depth**:

| Feature | Sayari | WireScreen |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Geographic focus | Global | China-centric |
| Entity coverage | Broad corporate registries | 14M+ Chinese companies |
| Unique value | Global supply chain mapping | Chinese military/state affiliations |
| BIS product | BIS50 module | BIS 50% Rule collections |

For export control enforcement targeting China specifically—semiconductors, AI chips, dual-use technology—WireScreen provides specialized intelligence Sayari cannot match.

## What the Contract Tells Us

### "Platform Access" - What BIS Got

The contract description suggests BIS purchased **evaluation access** to the WireScreen platform. The single-day performance period (October 7, 2024) indicates:

1. **Pilot/trial license**: Testing the platform's utility for BIS investigations
2. **Complementary tool**: Filling gaps in existing Sayari/Palantir capabilities
3. **China-specific need**: Addressing the unique challenges of Chinese corporate intelligence

### Why Only $25K?

Several factors explain the modest engagement:

1. **Evaluation phase**: Similar to Sayari's initial $80K contracts in 2021, this appears to be a pilot
2. **Specific use case**: China-focused investigations rather than enterprise-wide screening
3. **Nascent company**: WireScreen (founded 2019) is newer and smaller than established vendors like Sayari
4. **Complementary role**: Supplements rather than replaces existing tools

### Contract Vehicle

The contract was issued through **NASA SEWP** (NNG15SD26B) via **Thundercat Technology, LLC** as reseller—the same small business reseller BIS uses for Palantir. This suggests:
- BIS has established procurement pathways for emerging data analytics vendors
- Thundercat serves as an aggregator for BIS's intelligence platform acquisitions
