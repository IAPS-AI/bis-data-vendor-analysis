## About Conflict Armament Research

### The Organization

[Conflict Armament Research (CAR)](https://www.conflictarm.com/) is a UK-based investigative organization founded in 2011 by **James Bevan**, a weapons specialist and former head of the United Nations Sanctions Monitoring Group on Côte d'Ivoire.

Unlike Sayari (a commercial company built for government clients), CAR operates more like an investigative NGO:
- **Primary funding**: European Union and German Government (not US)
- **Core mission**: Tracking illicit weapons in active conflict zones
- **Method**: Field investigators physically document weapons, trace serial numbers, interview sources
- **Output**: Public reports and the iTrace weapons database

### The iTrace System

[iTrace](https://www.conflictarm.com/itrace/) is CAR's flagship product - a global weapons tracking database funded primarily by the EU through multiple Council Decisions:
- **2013**: Initial funding (Decision 2013/698/CFSP)
- **2015**: iTrace II
- **2017**: iTrace III
- **2019**: iTrace IV
- **2023**: iTrace V (€6.2 million allocation)

The system combines:
1. **Field documentation**: Teams document weapons at point of use in conflict zones
2. **Database tracking**: Serial numbers, manufacturers, and supply chains mapped globally
3. **Diversion monitoring**: Identifies when legally exported weapons end up with unauthorized users

Since 2014, CAR has documented **over 130,000 illicit weapons** across 15+ countries including Iraq, Syria, Libya, Somalia, and Ukraine.

## Why BIS Purchased iTrace Access

### The Iran-Russia Drone Connection

The timing of the BIS contract (February 2021) predates what would become CAR's most consequential investigation: tracking Western components in Iranian drones used by Russia in Ukraine.

Starting in late 2022, CAR investigations revealed:

> "Four UAS recovered in Ukraine—one Shahed-131, two Shahed-136, and one Mohajer-6 UAS—were made almost exclusively of components produced by companies based in Asia, Europe, and the United States... **82% of components were manufactured by companies based in the United States**."

Key findings:
- A single Shahed-136 drone contained parts from **13 different American companies**
- **56% of components** were manufactured in 2020-2021 (recent production, not old stockpiles)
- Original serial numbers had been **deliberately erased** to hide supply chains
- Post-invasion (2022+) components were found in drones, indicating **ongoing sanctions evasion**

This directly informs BIS's core mission: identifying export control violations and closing loopholes.

### The White House Task Force

CAR's findings were significant enough that:
> "In 2022, the Biden Administration launched a task force headed by the White House National Security Council to investigate how U.S.- and Western-origin components are ending up in Iranian-made UAVs that Russia is using in the war in Ukraine."

The BIS response included:
- New controls targeting Iranian UAVs
- Expanded EAR99 items controlled for export to Iran
- A new Iran Foreign Direct Product Rule (FDPR)

## What the Contract Tells Us

### "Portal Web Tracking Licenses" - What BIS Got

The contract description suggests BIS purchased access to the **iTrace Portal** - the web interface to CAR's weapons database. This would allow BIS analysts to:

1. **Search weapons by serial number**: If a controlled item turns up in a conflict zone, trace where it came from
2. **Identify diversion patterns**: Which countries/intermediaries are vectors for illicit arms flows
3. **Support enforcement cases**: Physical evidence for prosecution of export control violations

### Why Only One Contract?

Several possible explanations for the limited engagement:

1. **Free public access**: Much of iTrace data is publicly available; BIS may have gotten what they needed from the initial purchase
2. **EU-funded alternative**: Since the EU primarily funds CAR, US agencies may get indirect access through allied channels
3. **Different use case**: Unlike Sayari (entity screening at scale), CAR provides investigative evidence for specific cases - less need for ongoing bulk access
4. **Complementary tools**: BIS may use CAR for weapons-specific intelligence while Sayari handles corporate entity screening

## Trajectory Analysis

### The Engagement Pattern: One-and-Done vs. Growing Partnership

| Metric | Sayari | CAR |
|--------|--------|-----|
| First BIS contract | Feb 2021 | Feb 2021 |
| Contracts to date | 4 | 1 |
| Total spend | $1.75M | $120K |
| Trend | 17x increase in FY24 | No follow-on |
| Business model | SaaS subscriptions | EU-funded NGO |
| Primary customers | US government | European governments |

### Why the Difference?

1. **Business model**: Sayari is a venture-backed company ($235M from TPG) actively pursuing US government contracts. CAR is primarily EU-funded and doesn't appear to actively market to US agencies.

2. **Use case fit**:
   - Sayari = **screening** (bulk, continuous, automated)
   - CAR = **investigation** (specific cases, physical evidence)

3. **Overlap with existing capabilities**: BIS already has access to weapons-related intelligence through DOD, State Department, and intelligence community channels. CAR provides supplementary open-source evidence.

4. **Geographic focus**: CAR's strength is conflict zones (Syria, Libya, Ukraine). Sayari's strength is corporate structures (China, Russia sanctions evasion).

## The Bigger Picture: CAR's Impact on Export Control Policy

Even without large US government contracts, CAR's research directly shapes BIS policy:

### Iran FDPR (Foreign Direct Product Rule)
CAR's documentation of US components in Shahed drones provided the evidentiary basis for:
- New Iran-specific export controls
- Expanded list of controlled components
- Justification for enforcement actions

### Component-Level Tracking
CAR's methodology of identifying specific parts (down to the serial number) has pushed the export control regime toward:
> "The list of critical components subject to additional export control and sanction bans has increased from 20 to 50 items in three years."

### Enforcement Cooperation
CAR works directly with Ukrainian officials on:
> "The development of cooperation to study the ways in which components used for the production of weapons get to Russia, Iran and North Korea, bypassing sanctions."
